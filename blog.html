The Expanding Scope of Writ Jurisdiction in Protecting Fundamental Rights

Barrister M. Hasan Chowdhury 16 October 2025 Constitutional Law

The High Court Division's exercise of its jurisdiction under Article 102 of the Constitution of Bangladesh is one of the most critical mechanisms for upholding the rule of law and safeguarding citizens' fundamental rights. Recent judicial trends show a clear inclination towards a more expansive and purposive interpretation of this jurisdiction, moving beyond mere procedural review to addressing substantive injustice.

This is particularly evident in matters concerning environmental protection, public accountability, and administrative decisions affecting large sections of society. The courts are increasingly willing to look into the merits of governmental or public body actions when they infringe upon the basic guarantees enshrined in the Constitution.

Key Principles Driving Judicial Activism

Several core principles underpin this active approach. These principles ensure that the 'guardianship' role of the superior courts remains effective in a developing democracy:

  1. **Locus Standi Relaxation:** The traditional barriers to bringing public interest litigation (PIL) have been significantly eased, allowing affected groups or even concerned citizens to approach the court when public interest is at stake.
  2. **Doctrine of Legitimate Expectation:** This doctrine is frequently employed to ensure that public authorities act fairly and consistently, preventing arbitrary changes in policy that negatively impact citizens who relied on previous conduct.
  3. **Proportionality and Reasonableness:** The courts scrutinize administrative actions to ensure they are proportionate to the goal sought and reasonable in their effect on individuals' rights.
"The power vested in the High Court Division under Article 102 is not a mere power to issue directions, but a solemn obligation to enforce the constitutional mandate and the fundamental rights that form the bedrock of our republic."

For corporations operating in Bangladesh, understanding the implications of Writ Jurisdiction is vital. Challenges to regulatory decisions, tender processes, or tax assessments often find their way to the High Court under Article 102, demanding timely and expert legal intervention. Proactive compliance and strategic advisory services are paramount to mitigate risks arising from administrative overreach.

To navigate this complex landscape, legal strategies must be agile, informed by the latest judicial precedents, and focused on both procedural correctness and substantive justice.
